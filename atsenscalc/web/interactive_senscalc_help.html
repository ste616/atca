Help: Image Weighting

The sensitivity calculator uses Robust weighting only when computing the image weighting factors, since Robust weighting is preferable over Natural and Uniform schemes. When using Robust weighting, Robust=2 is essentially Natural weighting, and Robust=-2 is essentially Uniform. The weighting factor shown in the image parameters table describes exactly how the image RMS noise would compare to a traditionally Natural-weighted image. These factors have been determined using simulations over a range of frequencies and hour-angle ranges.

Help: Weather Conditions

The weather conditions used by the sensitivity calculator are derived from a study into the variations in temperature, pressure and humidity observed at the ATCA site over the past five years. The atmospheric opacity was derived from these parameters, and this was used to select the best, worst and typical conditions used by the calculator for each season that can be selected. You should expect that although you can have the "best" weather conditions for a short time during your observations, you will probably never reach the sensitivity shown in the "best" case. You should also always do better than the "worst" case. The sensitivity shown for "typical" weather conditions should be very close to what you would get for a normal observation in the selected season.

Help: RMS noise level

The RMS noise level computed by the sensitivity calculator is:
$\sigma_{\rm rms} = \frac{\sqrt{2} k {\rm T}_{\rm meas} W}{10^{-26} A_{\rm ant} \epsilon \sqrt{N_{\rm ant}(N_{\rm ant} - 1) \Delta\nu \tau}}$,
where
  • $\sigma_{\rm rms}$ is the RMS noise level, in units of mJy/beam,
  • $k$ is the Boltzmann constant, in units of ${\rm J}.{\rm K}^{-1}$,
  • ${\rm T}_{\rm meas}$ is the system temperature, in units of K,
  • $W$ is the image weighting factor (where for Natural weighting $W=1$),
  • $A_{\rm ant}$ is the surface area of a single antenna, in units of ${\rm m}^{2}$,
  • $\epsilon$ is the efficiency of the antenna (where for a perfect antenna $\epsilon=1$),
  • $N_{\rm ant}$ is the number of antenna in the array,
  • $\Delta\nu$ is the bandwidth, in units of MHz,
  • $\tau$ is the integration time, in units of seconds.

This gives the sensitivity to an unpolarised point source at the phase centre.

Help: Brightness Temperature Sensitivity

The brightness temperature sensitivity computed by the calculator is:
$\sigma_{\rm BT} = \frac{10^{-26}\sigma_{\rm rms}\lambda^{2}}{2k} \frac{(180/\pi)^{2}}{\pi \theta_{\rm min} \theta_{\rm maj}}$,
where
  • $\sigma_{\rm BT}$ is the brightness temperature sensitivity, in units of K,
  • $\sigma_{\rm rms}$ is the RMS noise level, in units of Jy/beam,
  • $\lambda$ is the wavelength, in units of m,
  • $k$ is the Boltzmann constant, in units of ${\rm J}.{\rm K}^{-1}$,
  • $\theta_{\rm min}$ is the semi-minor axis length of the synthesised beam, in units of degrees,
  • $\theta_{\rm maj}$ is the semi-major axis length of the synthesised beam, in units of degrees.
